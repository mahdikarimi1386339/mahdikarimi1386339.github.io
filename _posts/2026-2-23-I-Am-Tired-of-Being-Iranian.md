I am tired. I am tired of seeing people arrested simply for speaking against the state. I am tired of the endless hunt for a working VPN configuration just to access the "real" internet. I am tired of being here, and I am tired of what I’ve become here.

I am exhausted by our dangerously subpar cars and our unbelievably brutal society. I have grown weary of our traditions—from Nowruz to Ramadan—as they feel hollow in the face of our reality. Most of all, I am tired of being silent. I wasn't always this way, but the weight has become too much. So, let me write.

## The Illusion of Help
I don't want help. I’ve seen those who have condemned themselves by seeking help from others, and I realize now: I only need myself. I refuse to be further ruined by the news—which is almost always grim—or distracted by social media and games that my PC can barely run on its lowest settings.

When I open any social app, I don't see a single truly happy Iranian, anywhere in the world. It feels as though people are ready to drink each other's blood; a disgusting cycle of brutality and anger that no one seems able to break. I want to be more than just "flesh" that is no longer living. There is a specific, sharp pain in watching the world—or those who claim shared values—ignore or even support the forces that kill Iranians in the name of law. But I know the truth: we are on our own.

## The Constant Shadow
If you are not in Iran, you cannot truly understand the pervasive sense of fear we carry every day. It is the feeling that at any moment, someone could burst into your home and take you away for something you said.

What is worse than the fear of the state is the fear of being forgotten by your own family—or worse, being betrayed by those who still support the regime. If you don't keep your mouth shut, they will shut it for you. The massive wave of arrests that followed the terrible events of **January 2026** hasn't stopped. It continues to this day. You aren't even notified if your own classmates have been taken to jail. A heavy, suffocating silence is spreading across the country. From monarchists to republicans, no one is safe. We never have been.

## Final Thoughts
I don't know if I will survive this chapter of history. I just know I can no longer tolerate it. Every hour, every minute, I am haunted by "what ifs." What if a war starts? What if we become a nation of displaced people, wandering the world as "illegal" immigrants? What if this mess simply continues forever?

I am tired of wondering. I am just tired.
